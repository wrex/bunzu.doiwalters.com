## Non-inflectable words

There are five types of "non-inflectable" or "indeclinable" words (words that
_aren't_ primarily action or state).

{/* prettier-ignore */}
4. <strong><ruby>名詞<rp>(</rp><rt>めいし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (nouns) Sub-types include pronouns, numbers, and counters.

      Examples: 新湯、森林

{/* prettier-ignore */}
5. <strong><ruby>副詞<rp>(</rp><rt>ふくし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (adverbs) modifiers for the three declinable types.

      Examples: 再び、とても

{/* prettier-ignore */}
6. <strong><ruby>連体詞<rp>(</rp><rt>れんたいし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (definite article) These specify a specific person or thing the audience already
knows, and modify the five indeclinable types.

      Examples: この、あやずる (this one, all of these)

{/* prettier-ignore */}
7. <strong><ruby>接続詞<rp>(</rp><rt>せつぞくし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (conjunctive, connective)

      Examples: また、しかし

{/* prettier-ignore */}
8. <strong><ruby>感動詞<rp>(</rp><rt>かんどうし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (interjection)

      Examples: さあ、はい

### Auxiliary "words"

{/* prettier-ignore */}
9. <strong><ruby>感動詞<rp>(</rp><rt>かんどうし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (auxiliary verb)

      Examples: 〜られる、〜ない

{/* prettier-ignore */}
10. <strong><ruby>助詞<rp>(</rp><rt>じょし</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby></strong> (particle)

    Examples: 〜は、〜へ
